These notes survey musical theatre as its own historical tradition rather than as a side branch of opera or popular song. The course begins with minstrelsy, vaudeville, and operetta, then moves through Tin Pan Alley, *Show Boat*, Rodgers and Hammerstein, Bernstein, Sondheim, rock musicals, megamusicals, Disney, and *Hamilton* before turning to the rise of contemporary Mandarin musical theatre.

Along the way, the course treats the musical as a hybrid form where song, dialogue, dance, commerce, and stagecraft constantly reshape one another. The notes also build a comparative framework for understanding how musicals differ from opera and pop music while tracing how Broadway aesthetics travel across media and across cultures.
